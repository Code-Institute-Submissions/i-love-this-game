About


If you're of a certain age group (my age group), you were a child in the 80s and a teenager in the 90s. It was a great time to be alive - great movies, TV shows and cartoons, awesome music, funny fashion trends and true heroes. Everything was fascinating, and the world ran on the dreams of both adults and children. There were drugs and campaigns to keep us away from them, there was AIDS and campaigns to teach people how to protect themselves from it, there was hunger in Africa and campaigns to help ending it, and there were yuppies… Well, I never said it was perfect.

It was also an era of unforgettable sports moments - and never more so than in the NBA (the National Basketball Association), the professional basketball league in North America, the best professional basketball league in the world and, let's be honest, the greatest show on Earth.

We all remember the slogans “NBA Action: It's Fantastic” in the 80s and “I love This Game” in the 90s (this blog was named after the latter as an attempt by its creator to pay homage to these great promotional phrases and the memorable TV commercials that came with them).

We also remember the NBA of the 80s and 90s being an explosion of abnormally long appendages, short shorts, giant teeth, bad hairdos, moustaches and hairy armpits. I miss those armpits.

But, most importantly, the NBA of the 80s and 90s was the golden era of basketball. From 1979, when Magic Johnson and Larry Bird joined the league and the three-point shot was introduced and paved the way to the modern game, to 1998, when Michael Jordan retired (for the second time) and the Bulls dynasty ended, some of the best basketball moments, games, teams, players and coaches unfolded before our eyes, in a majestic display of skill, strength, bravery, excitement, humor, grace and sheer humanity. The players of this era were idols of millions but, unlike today's players, they were bigger than life. They were warriors. They were truly our heroes.

This blog was created to celebrate 80s and 90s basketball, a time when life was simpler, the world was a better place and kids like me would spend their days dreaming of dunking off an alley-oop in the Madison Square Garden, making the buzzer-beater off an assist by Bird in the Boston Garden, helping Magic running Showtime in the Forum or helping Jordan and Pippen win another playoff game in the United Center.

May this blog be a nostalgic, but beautiful trip down memory lane and a celebration of the two most exciting decades of basketball ever. And may basketball always be with you.
